\section{Staged refinement workflow (expanded)}
\label{sec:si_workflow}

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Beam characterization (direct beam).}
  Acquire direct-beam images at several detector distances.
  Fit the spot position/shape versus distance to determine the incident-beam widths
  $(w_x,w_z)$ and Gaussian divergences $(\Delta\theta_x,\Delta\theta_z)$ that set the
  instrument-limited broadening.

  \item \textbf{Detector geometry calibration (3D powder standard).}
  Measure a polycrystalline calibrant (e.g., hBN).
  Fit multiple Debye--Scherrer rings in detector coordinates and refine detector tilts
  $(\beta,\gamma)$, in-plane detector rotation $\chi$, and beam center $(x_0,y_0)$ so the rings
  forward-project to concentric circles (equivalently, ellipses in the raw detector frame are
  corrected to circles).

  \item \textbf{Sample alignment and goniometer misalignment (2D powder film).}
  Use the 2D-powder signature (paired symmetric in-plane features and single specular
  features) to seed alignment.
  With detector parameters fixed, refine sample alignment by minimizing positional residuals
  between measured and simulated features spanning specular and off-specular regions.
  Refine $(\theta_i,\delta,z_B,z_S)$ and the goniometer-axis misalignment rotations $(\alpha,\psi)$;
  include film dimensions $(W,H)$ and thickness $t$ to weight footprint and absorption.

  \item \textbf{Mosaicity from line shapes (two $\theta_i$ images).}
  Acquire two images at different $\theta_i$ and fit them simultaneously using a shared
  out-of-plane misorientation kernel
  \[
    \omega(\Delta\theta)=
    \eta\,\frac{1}{\pi}\frac{\Gamma}{\Delta\theta^2+\Gamma^2}+
    (1-\eta)\,\frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}\sigma}\exp\!\left(-\frac{\Delta\theta^2}{2\sigma^2}\right),
    \qquad 0\le \eta \le 1,
  \]
  (use the $2\pi$-periodic wrapped form on $(-\pi,\pi]$; see Sec.~S1.1).
  Determine parameters from arc/cap widths and specular off-condition tails after normalizing
  an overall scale. Report Lorentzian FWHM $=2\Gamma$, Gaussian FWHM $=2\sqrt{2\ln 2}\,\sigma$,
  and mixing factor $\eta$.

  \item \textbf{Structure-factor constrained intensities (two $\theta_i$ images).}
  With geometry and mosaicity fixed, refine the intensity model against the full 2D images
  (including specular).
  Evaluate all allowed reciprocal vectors $G$ explicitly and use $|F_G|^2$ to set reflection
  strengths.
  Refine an overall scale plus structure-factor parameters (occupancies, anisotropic
  Debye--Waller terms, and allowed static displacements) to match reflection-to-reflection
  intensity ratios across both images.

  \item \textbf{Stacking and polytype disorder (diffuse along $Q_z$).}
  After the ordered model converges, introduce stacking-disorder (layer-slip / polytype)
  parameters and refine them against the diffuse intensity between Bragg peaks along $Q_z$
  (diffuse intensity along specular / Bragg rods and its $Q_z$-dependence), e.g., with an
  Hendricks--Teller-type model when appropriate.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Wrapped mosaic distribution}
\label{subsec:si_wrapped_mosaic}

In the main text we describe out-of-plane mosaicity by a misorientation density
$\omega(\Delta\theta)$ in the crystallite tilt angle $\Delta\theta$ relative to the substrate
normal. Since $\Delta\theta$ is an orientation angle, $\Delta\theta$ and $\Delta\theta+2\pi$
represent the same physical state; accordingly, $\omega$ is most naturally treated as a
$2\pi$-periodic (``wrapped'') probability density on $\Delta\theta\in(-\pi,\pi]$.
Without wrapping, an otherwise smooth kernel on the real line produces an artificial seam at
$\pm\pi$ and assigns different weights to physically equivalent orientations separated by $2\pi$,
which can bias the weak tail intensity used to constrain mosaicity.

We use a wrapped pseudo-Voigt kernel,
\[
  \omega_{\mathrm w}(\Delta\theta;\eta,\Gamma,\sigma)
  =\eta\,L_{\mathrm w}(\Delta\theta;\Gamma)+(1-\eta)\,G_{\mathrm w}(\Delta\theta;\sigma),
  \qquad 0\le \eta \le 1,
\]
where the wrapped Lorentzian and wrapped Gaussian may be written by periodic summation (equivalently,
in closed form) as
\[
  L_{\mathrm w}(\Delta\theta;\Gamma)=
  \sum_{m=-\infty}^{\infty}\frac{1}{\pi}\frac{\Gamma}{(\Delta\theta+2\pi m)^2+\Gamma^2}
  =\frac{1}{2\pi}\,\frac{\sinh\Gamma}{\cosh\Gamma-\cos\Delta\theta},
\]
\[
  G_{\mathrm w}(\Delta\theta;\sigma)=
  \sum_{m=-\infty}^{\infty}\frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}\sigma}
  \exp\!\left[-\frac{(\Delta\theta+2\pi m)^2}{2\sigma^2}\right]
  =\frac{1}{2\pi}\!\left[1+2\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}e^{-n^2\sigma^2/2}\cos(n\Delta\theta)\right]
  =\frac{1}{2\pi}\,\theta_{3}\!\left(\frac{\Delta\theta}{2},e^{-\sigma^2/2}\right).
\]
Both integrate to unity over $(-\pi,\pi]$, so $\omega_{\mathrm w}$ is normalized. In the
narrow-width regime relevant here ($\Gamma,\sigma\ll\pi$), $\omega_{\mathrm w}$ reduces locally
near $\Delta\theta=0$ to the familiar unwrapped Lorentzian/Gaussian used in the main text, and we
report Lorentzian FWHM $=2\Gamma$ and Gaussian FWHM $=2\sqrt{2\ln 2}\,\sigma$.

For a reflection of Bragg-sphere radius $R$, mosaicity redistributes intensity in polar angle
$\vartheta$ with meridional line density $I_0\,\omega(\vartheta)\,d\vartheta$. Random in-plane
rotations about the film normal revolve this line density about the $G_z$ axis, yielding an axially
symmetric surface density $\sigma(\vartheta)$. With area element
$dA=2\pi R^2\sin\vartheta\,d\vartheta$, conservation of intensity gives
\[
  \sigma(\vartheta)=\frac{I_0}{2\pi R^2\sin\vartheta}\,\omega(\vartheta),
  \qquad 0<\vartheta<\pi,
\]
as used in the main text.

\section
{ Material background and stacking–fault model}

In layered metal–halides such as PbI$_2$ each I–Pb–I sandwich behaves like a rigid trilayer that can shift laterally with negligible energy cost compared with the much stronger intra‑layer bonding.  Consequently the crystal can adopt several
{polytypes} that differ only in the {stacking sequence} of these sandwiches along the $c$ axis.
Here we focus on the two sequences that bracket the experimentally relevant window:

\subsection{Polytypism of PbI\texorpdfstring{$_2$}{2}}
\label{subsec:polytype}
\begin{description}
  \item[2H (\textit{P}$\bar3$m1)] AB repeat stable below $140\,^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$; no lateral shift ($\phi_{AB}=0$); see Fig.~\ref{fig:2H}.
  \item[6H (\textit{R}$\bar3$)] ABCA repeat above $\approx 200\,^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$; lateral shift $a/\sqrt3$ gives $\phi=2\pi/3$; 2H $\Leftrightarrow$ 6H transition near $94\,^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$; see Fig.~\ref{fig:6H}.
\end{description}

Our samples never achieve perfect periodicity; stacks often contain finite 2H and 6H "domains" interspersed by {stacking faults}. To model the resulting diffuse scattering we adapt the Hendricks--Teller (HT) Markov formalism and follow the streamlined matrix notation introduced a few years later by Lifshitz, who showed how to re-express pair probabilities through the eigenvalues of a $3\times3$ transition matrix.

\subsection{Molecular structure factor for PbI\texorpdfstring{$_2$}{2}}

A single layer form factor is 
\begin{equation}
    F_m = b_{Pb} + b_I \left[ e^{2\pi i \left( H/3 + 2K/3 + Lz/3 \right) } +e^{2\pi i \left( 2H/3 + K/3 - Lz/3 \right) } \right]
\end{equation}

Where now the amplitude will be 
\begin{equation}
    A = F_m \sum_{R_p} e^{i \vec{Q} \cdot \vec{R_p}} 
    \label{eq:FF}
\end{equation}

Where $\vec{R_p} = m\vec{a} + n\vec{b}$ is the in-plane position vector, with $m,n\in\mathbb{Z}$.
Then $\vec{Q}\cdot\vec{R_p} \rightarrow Hm + kn$, and the Bragg condition makes $H,K$ integers.


\section{No‑correlation stacking‑fault model}\label{sec:nocorr}

Let $\tau_j\in\{0,1\}$ indicate whether the $j$‑th interface slips $(\tau_j=1)$ or not $(\tau_j=0)$.  
The structure factor of one layer is $F_0(h,k,\ell)$, and for a three‑layer (ABC) repeat the per‑layer phase advance is  
\begin{equation}
  \phi(h,k,\ell)=\frac{h+2k}{3}+\ell/3,
  \label{eq:phi_def}
\end{equation}
with $h,k,\ell$ in reciprocal‑lattice units.
\\
We now add the phase of each layer to the form factor from equation \ref{eq:FF}.
\begin{equation}
    A = F_m \sum_{R_p} e^{i \vec{Q} \cdot \vec{R_p}} \sum_\ell e^{i \phi(HKL) \ell}
\end{equation}
Writing $e^{i \phi \ell} = \prod_{j=1}^\ell e^{i \phi}$

\begin{equation}
    A = F_m \sum_{R_p} e^{i \vec{Q} \cdot \vec{R_p}} \prod_\ell e^{i \phi(HKL) \ell}
\end{equation}


\subsection{Coherent amplitude of an \texorpdfstring{$N$}{N}-layer stack}
At layer $\ell$ the factor multiplying $F_0$ is
\[
  \bigl[1-\tau_\ell+\tau_\ell e^{-i\phi}\bigr]\,e^{i\phi\ell},
\]
so that  
\begin{equation}
  A
  =F_0\sum_{\ell=0}^{N-1}
        \left[\prod_{j=0}^{\ell}
               \bigl(1-\tau_j+\tau_j e^{-i\phi}\bigr)\right]
        e^{i\phi\ell}.
  \label{eq:A_full}
\end{equation}

\subsection{Uncorrelated faults}
Assume the $\tau_j$ are i.i.d.\ with slip probability $p=\Pr(\tau_j=1)$. Independence gives  
\begin{equation}
  \Bigl\langle\prod_{j=0}^{\ell}
           (1-\tau_j+\tau_j e^{-i\phi})\Bigr\rangle
  =\bigl[(1-p)+p\,e^{-i\phi}\bigr]^{\ell}
  \equiv f^{\,\ell},
  \qquad f\equiv(1-p)+p\,e^{-i\phi}.
  \label{eq:f_def}
\end{equation}

\subsection{Mean amplitude}
Averaging \eqref{eq:A_full} and inserting \eqref{eq:f_def} gives the geometric series  
\begin{equation}
  \langle A\rangle
  =F_0\sum_{\ell=0}^{N-1}f^{\ell}e^{i\phi\ell}
  =F_0\frac{1-(f\,e^{i\phi})^{N}}{1-f\,e^{i\phi}}.
  \label{eq:A_mean_N}
\end{equation}

\subsection{Infinite‑crystal limit}
For any non‑zero fault probability ($|f|<1$),
\begin{equation}
  \boxed{\;
    \lim_{N\to\infty}\langle A\rangle
    =F_0\,\frac{f}{1-f\,e^{i\phi}}
  \;}
  \label{eq:A_mean_inf}
\end{equation}
The perfect‑crystal result is recovered as $f\!\to\!1$.

\subsection{Mean‑square amplitude}
The heights of sharp Bragg peaks: i.e. the square of the coherently averaged structure amplitude. This is only the part of the intensity that survives perfect phase coherence: sharp Bragg peaks. Any layer-to-layer fluctuations are averaged before squaring and thus disappear. 

\begin{align}
  |\langle A\rangle|^{2}
  &=|F_0|^{2}\frac{f^{2}}{|1-f\,e^{i\phi}|^{2}}
     \notag\\[4pt]
  &=|F_0|^{2}\frac{f^{2}}{(1-f\cos\phi)^{2}+f^{2}\sin^{2}\phi}
     \label{eq:intensity_cos_sin}\\[4pt]
  &=|F_0|^{2}\frac{f^{2}}{(1-f)^{2}+4f\sin^{2}\!\dfrac{\phi}{2}}.
     \label{eq:intensity_halfangle}
\end{align}

\subsection{ Ensemble-averaged intensity}
The full detector signal; adds the broadened peaks and diffuse background arising from stacking faults.
\begin{equation}
    <|A|^2>
\end{equation}
\subsubsection{Layer Autocorrelation}
The layer-resolved autocorrelation of the stacking sequence tells you how phase coherence decays with layer phase shifts. It is the kernel that converts local disorder into the full intensity giving both Bragg and diffuse components. 
\\

Define
\begin{equation}
  g_j \equiv 1-\tau_j + \tau_j e^{-i\phi}, 
  \qquad
  S_{\ell} \equiv \prod_{j=0}^{\ell} g_j.
\end{equation}
We wish to compute
\begin{equation}
  \bigl\langle S_{\ell}S_{\ell'}^{*}\bigr\rangle
  =\bigl\langle \prod_{j=0}^{\ell}g_j \;\prod_{j'=0}^{\ell'}g_{j'}^{*}\bigr\rangle.
\end{equation}
Since the $g_j$ are i.i.d., expectations of products factor, and $g_jg_j^{*}=1$ identically.  We split into two cases:

\paragraph{Case A: $\ell\ge\ell'$.}
\begin{equation}
S_{\ell}S_{\ell'}^{*}
\;=\;
\underbrace{\Bigl(\prod_{j=0}^{\ell'}g_j\Bigr)
            \Bigl(\prod_{j'=0}^{\ell'}g_{j'}^{*}\Bigr)}_{\displaystyle =\,1\;\;\text{(common layers)}}
\;
\underbrace{\Bigl(\prod_{j=\ell'+1}^{\ell}g_j\Bigr)}_{\text{extra layers}}
\end{equation}
- For $0\le j\le\ell'$, $g_jg_j^{*}=1$, so the first two products give 1.  
- For $j=\ell'+1,\dots,\ell$, independence implies
\begin{equation}
  \Bigl\langle\prod_{j=\ell'+1}^{\ell}g_j\Bigr\rangle
  =\prod_{j=\ell'+1}^{\ell}\langle g_j\rangle
  =f^{\,\ell-\ell'}.
\end{equation}
Hence
\begin{equation}
  \bigl\langle S_{\ell}S_{\ell'}^{*}\bigr\rangle
  =f^{\,\ell-\ell'},
  \qquad(\ell\ge\ell').
\end{equation}

\paragraph{Case B: $\ell<\ell'$.}
By an identical argument, swapping roles,
\[
  \bigl\langle S_{\ell}S_{\ell'}^{*}\bigr\rangle
  =\bigl(f^{*}\bigr)^{\ell'-\ell},
  \qquad(\ell<\ell').
\]

\paragraph{The Solutions are then}
\begin{equation}
  \boxed{
  \bigl\langle S_{\ell}S_{\ell'}^{*}\bigr\rangle
  =
  \begin{cases}
    f^{\,\ell-\ell'}, & \ell\ge\ell',\\[6pt]
    \bigl(f^{*}\bigr)^{\ell'-\ell}, & \ell<\ell'.
  \end{cases}
  }
  \label{eq:two_point}
\end{equation}

\subsection{Single‑sum form of the double series}
With $\ell''=\ell-\ell'$, the double sum becomes  
\begin{equation}
  \sum_{\ell=0}^{N_z-1}\sum_{\ell'=0}^{N_z-1}
     f^{|\ell-\ell'|}e^{i(\phi-\psi)(\ell-\ell')}
  =N_z\sum_{\ell''=-N_z+1}^{N_z-1}
        f^{|\ell''|}e^{i(\phi-\psi)\ell''}.
  \label{eq:double_sum}
\end{equation}
Splitting out $\ell''=0$ and the $\pm\ell''$ pairs yields  
\[
  N_z\!\left[
    1+\sum_{\ell''=1}^{N_z-1}
      f^{\ell''}\bigl(
        e^{i(\phi-\psi)\ell''}+e^{-i(\phi-\psi)\ell''}
      \bigr)
  \right],
\]
with  
\[
  \sum_{\ell''=1}^{N_z-1}f^{\ell''}e^{\pm i(\phi-\psi)\ell''}
  =\frac{f\,e^{\pm i(\phi-\psi)}
         \bigl[1-f^{N_z-1}e^{\pm i(\phi-\psi)(N_z-1)}\bigr]}
        {1-f\,e^{\pm i(\phi-\psi)}}.
\]

\subsection{Infinite-crystal limit (\texorpdfstring{$0\le f<1$}{0<=f<1})}
Letting $N_z\!\to\!\infty$ gives  
\begin{equation}
  \sum_{\ell,\ell'}f^{|\ell-\ell'|}e^{i(\phi-\psi)(\ell-\ell')}
  =N_z\,\frac{1-f^{2}}{1+f^{2}-2f\cos(\phi-\psi)}.
  \label{eq:sum_infinite}
\end{equation}

\subsection{Scattering cross‑section}
Substituting \eqref{eq:sum_infinite},
\begin{equation}
  \boxed{
    \frac{d\sigma}{d\Omega}
    =|F_m|^{2}\,
      \frac{(2\pi)^{2}}{A_c}\,
      \delta\bigl(\mathbf{Q}-\mathbf{G}_p\bigr)\,
      N_p N_z\,
      \frac{1-f^{2}}{1+f^{2}-2f\cos(\phi-\psi)}
  }.
  \label{eq:cross_section}
\end{equation}
This is identical to the Hendrix–Teller formula upon identifying 
$\phi^{\text{HT}}=\phi-\psi$ and noting that their parameter 
$\cos(\phi^{1}-\phi^{2})$ equals $f$.
