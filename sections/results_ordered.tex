\section{Results: ordered films}

We first validate the forward model on the ordered van der Waals films, where the dominant
task is to reproduce the measured detector geometry, reflection positions, and relative
intensities with the crystallographic structure held fixed once the geometry and mosaicity
have been determined. This step is the cleanest test of whether the simulation is capturing
the mapping from reciprocal space to the detector, because the sharp Bragg features in
Bi$_2$Se$_3$ and Bi$_2$Te$_3$ are sensitive to both the calibrated geometry and the ordered
structure factor.

Figure~\ref{fig:ordered_comp} compares representative experimental and calculated 2D WAXS
images for the ordered films. The simulation reproduces the main Bragg-peak locations and the
overall distribution of intensity across the specular and off-specular regions. Any remaining
discrepancies are confined to localized regions near individual reflections and do not change
the global pattern of agreement. In particular, the same refined geometry used in the
simulation places the reflections in the correct detector positions without additional
post-hoc shifts.

The quantitative refinement is then summarized through line cuts across representative
reflections, as shown in Fig.~\ref{fig:ordered_linecuts}. These cuts emphasize that the
refined model matches not only the integrated intensity but also the reflection shape along
$Q_z$, which is the most sensitive direction for the layered ordering problem. The fitted scale
and structure-factor parameters are summarized in Table~\ref{tab:ordered_fit_summary}. The key
result is that a physically constrained ordered model explains the measured intensity ratios
with a single internally consistent set of parameters.

Taken together, the ordered-film results establish that the forward model captures the
instrumental projection, the reciprocal-space mapping, and the average ordered structure
well enough to be used as the baseline for the disorder analysis in PbI$_2$.
