\section{Results: diffuse scattering and stacking disorder in PbI\texorpdfstring{$_2$}{2}}
\label{sec:results_pbi2}

PbI$_2$ extends the ordered-film analysis to a case where the detector image is not described by Bragg peaks from a single periodic stacking sequence alone.

We can explain this selective manifestation of diffuse intensity with stacking disorder I--Pb--I trilayers remain crystalline enough to define in-plane reciprocal-space radii, but the registry between adjacent trilayers is not perfectly periodic. That loss of stacking coherence redistributes intensity along $Q_z$ and produces diffuse features.

The PbI$_2$ extension is therefore introduced only after the ordered baseline. Beam and detector terms, sample alignment, mosaicity, profile broadening, lattice constants, and the ordered PbI$_2$ rod intensities are fixed first. The disorder model is then asked to explain the remaining systematic diffuse signal on selected m/L rods. This ordering is important because a diffuse feature should be interpreted as stacking information only after the detector geometry and orientation distribution have already been constrained by the ordered Bragg features.

For a two-dimensional powder film, random in-plane rotation turns each in-plane reflection family into a reciprocal-space cylinder at fixed $Q_R$. Perfect stacking would concentrate intensity at discrete ordered Bragg values of $Q_z$ on those cylinders. For the $PbI_2$ case we see tails in $2\theta$ between the Bragg peaks sharing the same m. That residual is the observational reason for adding a stacking-disorder term.


\subsection{Stacking motifs and polytypism}

The structural unit treated by the disorder model is the I--Pb--I trilayer. Ordered 2H-like and laterally shifted 6H-like motifs differ primarily in the basal translation accumulated from one trilayer to the next. This makes PbI$_2$ a natural case for a Hendricks--Teller-type rod treatment in which the ordered layer form factor is retained and the layer-to-layer correlation enters as an analytical correction along $L$.\cite{HendricksTeller1942}

For a basal translation $(\Delta x,\Delta y)$ in fractional hexagonal coordinates, a reflection $(h,k,L)$ acquires the phase
\begin{equation}
  \exp\!\left[i2\pi(h\Delta x+k\Delta y)\right].
  \label{eq:pbi2_translation_phase}
\end{equation}
The slip convention used here corresponds to the basal translation
\begin{equation}
  \mathbf{s}=\left(\frac{2}{3},\frac{1}{3}\right),
  \qquad
  hs_x+ks_y=\frac{2h+k}{3},
  \label{eq:pbi2_slip_vector}
\end{equation}
so the default PbI$_2$ slip phase is
\begin{equation}
  \delta(h,k)=2\pi\left(\frac{2h+k}{3}\right).
  \label{eq:pbi2_delta}
\end{equation}
If $\tau_j\in\{0,1\}$ marks whether interface $j$ slips, the cumulative basal offset of layer $n$ is
\begin{equation}
  \mathbf{u}_n=\left(\sum_{j=0}^{n-1}\tau_j\right)\mathbf{s}\pmod{1},
  \qquad n=0,\ldots,N-1.
  \label{eq:pbi2_cumulative_shift}
\end{equation}
Thus a no-slip interface leaves the next trilayer in the same lateral registry, while a slip interface advances the next trilayer by $\mathbf{s}$. The all-slip three-layer sequence gives offsets $(0,0)$, $(2/3,1/3)$, and $(1/3,2/3)$, then returns to $(0,0)$ on the fourth layer. This is the ABCA closure used to connect the reciprocal-space disorder phase to the 6H-like visual motif.

The active convention first remaps the reported parameter through
\begin{equation}
  p_{\mathrm{flip}}=1-p.
  \label{eq:pbi2_pflip}
\end{equation}
With the slip phase in Eq.~\eqref{eq:pbi2_delta}, the layer-step average is
\begin{equation}
  z=(1-p_{\mathrm{flip}})+p_{\mathrm{flip}}e^{i\delta},
  \qquad
  f=|z|,
  \qquad
  \psi=\arg z.
  \label{eq:pbi2_zfpsi}
\end{equation}
The out-of-plane phase sampled by the rod model is
\begin{equation}
  \phi(h,k,L)=\delta(h,k)+\frac{2\pi L}{\phi_{L,\mathrm{div}}},
  \label{eq:pbi2_phi}
\end{equation}
where $\phi_{L,\mathrm{div}}$ is the divisor defining the active $L$ phase convention. For an infinite stack, the Hendricks--Teller correction is
\begin{equation}
  R_{\infty}(h,k,L)=
  \frac{1-f^2}{1+f^2-2f\cos\!\left[\phi(h,k,L)-\psi\right]}.
  \label{eq:pbi2_rinf}
\end{equation}
The finite-domain form used when an explicit stack length is retained is
\begin{equation}
  R_N(h,k,L)=
  1+\frac{2}{N}\operatorname{Re}\!\sum_{n=1}^{N-1}(N-n)t^n,
  \qquad
  t=f\,e^{i[\phi(h,k,L)-\psi]}.
  \label{eq:pbi2_rn}
\end{equation}
Equation~\eqref{eq:pbi2_rn} approaches Eq.~\eqref{eq:pbi2_rinf} as $N\rightarrow\infty$. In the ordered limit, the correction collapses back toward the discrete Bragg sequence. As the effective layer-to-layer correlation decreases, coherent peak weight is redistributed into broader between-peak intensity along the same rod.

When several local stacking environments are allowed, the calculation constructs multiple Hendricks--Teller component curves and combines them with nonnegative raw weights $w_\mu$. After normalization,
\begin{equation}
  \tilde{w}_\mu=\frac{w_\mu}{\sum_\nu w_\nu},
  \qquad
  I^{\mathrm{mix}}_{hk}(L)=\sum_{\mu=1}^{M}\tilde{w}_\mu I^{(\mu)}_{hk}(L).
  \label{eq:pbi2_mixture}
\end{equation}
The weights are therefore interpreted as effective fractions within the selected model family, not literal macroscopic phase fractions. Before detector projection, rods are grouped by the in-plane radial class
\begin{equation}
  m=h^2+hk+k^2,
  \label{eq:pbi2_qrclass}
\end{equation}
because all reflections with the same $m$ share the same in-plane $Q_R$ magnitude in the two-dimensional powder average. Their intensity curves are interpolated onto a common $L$ grid and summed before the detector trace is launched.

\subsection{Selected-rod refinement and physical interpretation}

The selected-rod profile is fit directly. The known Bragg positions on the retained branch define the centers of the profile terms inherited from the fixed detector geometry and ordered structural model. The disorder parameters control the redistribution of intensity among those terms and the diffuse between-peak signal through Eqs.~\eqref{eq:pbi2_rod_main}--\eqref{eq:pbi2_rn}; peak amplitudes are not released as independent free parameters. This is necessary because independently fitting the peaks first would allow the disorder signal to be absorbed into empirical peak areas, widths, or local backgrounds before the stacking model is tested.

The residual minimized in this stage has the schematic form
\begin{equation}
  r_j=\frac{I_j^{\mathrm{meas}}-I_j^{\mathrm{model}}(\boldsymbol{\theta}_{\mathrm{dis}})}{\sigma_j},
  \label{eq:pbi2_disorder_profile_residual}
\end{equation}
where $\boldsymbol{\theta}_{\mathrm{dis}}$ contains the slip probability, finite-stack length, declared mixture weights, and any explicitly allowed nuisance scale or background terms. The fit is accepted only if it improves the selected rod profile without destroying the ordered detector-space agreement established by the upstream refinement stages.

The PbI$_2$ profile is broader and more Lorentzian dominated than the ordered-film benchmarks. That matters because long Lorentzian tails allow more than the expected specular conditions to be met, making off-specular PbI$_2$ Bragg intensity a natural consequence of the fitted orientation distribution. Those tails, however, are a global profile effect. They broaden Bragg features tied to the same orientation distribution, but they do not generate continuous intensity selectively along only certain $Q_z$ rods. The selective presence and absence of diffuse rods is therefore assigned to stacking disorder rather than to the ordered baseline, detector resolution, or mosaic tails alone.

The preliminary ordered-baseline and disorder parameters used for the selected-rod comparison are summarized in Table~\ref{tab:pbi2_sf_parameters}. These values should be read as selected-rod descriptors of the present model state rather than universal PbI$_2$ constants. The clean ordered baseline keeps the average structure stoichiometric, while the diffuse rod intensity is assigned to stacking correlations.

The physical interpretation is therefore deliberately limited. The fitted slip probability, finite-stack length, and 2H:6H mixture describe the stacking correlations required by the measured selected-rod intensity under the stated Hendricks--Teller convention. They are not a unique microscopic reconstruction of every trilayer sequence in the film. Their scientific value is that they quantify the minimum stacking-disorder physics needed once the ordered detector-space baseline has failed.
