\section{Results: diffuse scattering in PbI\texorpdfstring{$_2$}{2}}

We next applied the workflow to CVD-grown PbI$_2$ films. PbI$_2$ was the disorder-sensitive case because the measured image contained diffuse intensity along selected $Q_z$ rods that was not captured by the ordered baseline alone. The analysis began in the same way as for the ordered films: beam and detector terms were fixed, sample alignment was determined from detector-space feature positions, mosaic/profile parameters were constrained from local line shapes, and the ordered PbI$_2$ intensity model was fixed from the Bragg peak positions and areas. The stacking-fault model was introduced only after that ordered baseline was established.

The final selected-rod intensity was represented as
\begin{equation}
I_{hk}(L)=\mathcal{A}F_{hk}^{2}(L)R_{hk}(L;p,N,\ldots),
\label{eq:pbi2_rod_main}
\end{equation}
where $\mathcal{A}$ is the image-level scale factor, $F_{hk}^{2}(L)$ is the ordered PbI$_2$ rod intensity, $R_{hk}$ is the analytical stacking-fault correction, $p$ is the fitted slip or fault parameter, and $N$ is an effective finite-stack number. When multiple images are fit, $\mathcal{A}$ is shared within each image but is not constrained to be identical across images. The disorder observable was built from the scattering magnitude and from the polar angle $\eta$ of the scattering vector relative to the film normal,
\begin{equation}
Q=\frac{4\pi}{\lambda}\sin\!\left(\frac{2\theta}{2}\right),
\qquad
Q_r=|\sin\eta|Q,
\qquad
Q_z=\cos\eta\,Q.
\label{eq:pbi2_selected_coords}
\end{equation}
This is the same reciprocal-space transformation used in the caked selected-rod analysis; $\eta$ denotes the polar angle used for the $Q_r/Q_z$ conversion and is distinct from the detector-plane azimuth $\phi$ used in the detector-geometry discussion. A rod mask kept bins satisfying $|Q_r-Q_{r,0}|\le\Delta Q_r$ and the chosen $Q_z$ bounds, with one detector branch used for the default fit and the opposite branch held as a validation profile.

The selected-rod profile was fit directly. Beam, detector geometry, sample alignment, mosaic/profile state, lattice constants, and the ordered PbI$_2$ baseline were held fixed. Known Bragg positions on the retained branch defined the profile locations, and the number of film layers was used as an input to the stacking model. The free disorder terms were the stacking-fault slip probability, $p$, an effective stack length, $N$, and the relative ratio of the 2H and 6H stacking-sequences.

Figure~\ref{fig:pbi2_disorder_fit} shows the selected-rod comparison. The ordered baseline captured the principal Bragg locations but left systematic positive residual intensity along $Q_z$. Adding the analytical stacking-fault term reduced the selected-rod residual while leaving the upstream detector-space agreement intact. The fitted values were therefore interpreted as effective stacking-disorder descriptors for the rod and branch analyzed here.

The PbI$_2$ structure-factor parameters used for the ordered baseline in Fig.~\ref{fig:pbi2_disorder_fit} are summarized in Table~\ref{tab:pbi2_sf_parameters}. The lattice constants and iodine coordinate are tied to reported 2H structural values, and the near-full occupancies are tied to the clean ordered model. The displacement terms are retained as fitted sample-specific ADP values because transferable site-resolved Pb and I displacement values are not widely reported with a consistent convention across the PbI$_2$ literature. The fitted values nevertheless preserve the qualitative condition that Pb motion is larger perpendicular to the layer than within the layer.\cite{Minagawa1975,Palosz1990,Lin2019,Trueblood1996}
