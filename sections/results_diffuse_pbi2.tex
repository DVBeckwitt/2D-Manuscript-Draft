\section{Results: diffuse scattering in PbI\texorpdfstring{$_2$}{2}}

We next applied the workflow to CVD-grown PbI$_2$ films. PbI$_2$ was the disorder-sensitive case because the measured image contained diffuse intensity along selected $Q_z$ rods that was not captured by the ordered baseline alone. The analysis began in the same way as for the ordered films: beam and detector terms were fixed, sample alignment was determined from detector-space feature positions, mosaic/profile parameters were constrained from local line shapes, and the ordered PbI$_2$ intensity model was fixed from the Bragg peak positions and areas. The stacking-fault model was introduced only after that ordered baseline is complete.  

The final selected-rod intensity was represented as
\begin{equation}
I_{hk}(L)=\mathcal{A}F_{hk}^{2}(L)R_{hk}(L;p,N,\ldots),
\label{eq:pbi2_rod_main}
\end{equation}
where $F_{hk}^{2}(L)$ is the ordered PbI$_2$ rod intensity, $R_{hk}$ is the analytical stacking-fault correction, $p$ is the reported slip or fault parameter, and $N$ was retained only when an effective finite-stack length was used. The disorder observable was built from the scattering magnitude and selected-rod coordinates,
\begin{equation}
Q=\frac{4\pi}{\lambda}\sin\!\left(\frac{2\theta}{2}\right),
\qquad
Q_r=|\sin\phi|Q,
\qquad
Q_z=\cos\phi\,Q.
\label{eq:pbi2_selected_coords}
\end{equation}
A rod mask kept bins satisfying $|Q_r-Q_{r,0}|\le\Delta Q_r$ and the chosen $Q_z$ bounds, with one detector branch used for the default fit and the opposite branch held as a validation profile.

The selected-rod profile was fit directly. Beam, detector geometry, sample alignment, mosaic/profile state, lattice constants, and the ordered PbI$_2$ baseline were held fixed. Known Bragg positions on the retained branch defined the profile locations. Number of layers of the film is used. The free terms were the stacking-fault slipping probability (p) and ratio of the polytypes present.

(c) Selected-rod profile after adding the analytical stacking-disorder term while retaining the ordered baseline and upstream geometry/profile parameters.   \label{fig:pbi2_disorder_fit}

Figure~\ref{fig:pbi2_disorder_fit} shows the selected-rod comparison. The ordered baseline captured the principal Bragg locations but left systematic positive residual intensity along $Q_z$. Adding the analytical stacking-fault term reduced the selected-rod residual while leaving the upstream detector-space agreement intact. The fitted values were therefore interpreted as effective stacking-disorder descriptors for the rod and branch analyzed here.
