\section{Discussion and conclusion}
\label{sec:discussion_conclusion}

The ordered Bi$_2$Se$_3$ and Bi$_2$Te$_3$ films validate the mosaic model and detector-space forward model. A single calculation accounts for detector geometry, finite beam phase space, sample alignment, mosaicity, resolution, and ordered structure-factor weighting, then compares measured and calculated $Q_z$ profiles along the same fixed-$m$ trajectories. The key result is direct line-shape agreement.

Keeping the comparison in detector space is essential because the constraining intensity is distributed among off-specular arcs, cap-like $m=0$ features, and weak off-condition specular peaks that a conventional 1D reduction would obscure. Because the calculated image includes the same effects and is projected in the same way as the measurement, the overlay tests the complete experimental model. The off-condition $m=0$ peaks also show why a narrow Gaussian-like mosaic core is insufficient and why the Lorentzian tail is required.

The projected profiles should therefore be read as detector-derived, resolution-limited observables rather than exact cuts through an ideal reciprocal-space volume. The detector-pixel contribution to the transformed-coordinate uncertainty and the propagated intensity uncertainty are included to define this limitation, while the final comparison remains a like-for-like test because data and calculation pass through the same projection. A complete covariance or posterior analysis of every fitted structure parameter is not claimed here; the present claim is that the detector-space model reproduces the measured ordered-film line shapes closely enough to support the proposed refinement workflow.
