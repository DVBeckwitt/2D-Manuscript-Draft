\section{Discussion and conclusion}

The ordered Bi$_2$Te$_3$ and Bi$_2$Se$_3$ films provide the validation for both the mosaic model and forward modelling. A single detector-space workflow accounts for detector geometry, finite beam phase space, sample alignment, mosaicity, resolution, and ordered structure-factor intensity, then compares measured and calculated profiles along the same indexed $Q_r$/HK trajectories. The key result is the direct $Q_z$ line-shape agreement.

Keeping the comparison in detector space is essential because the information that constrains the model is distributed between arcs, caps, and weak off-condition specular peaks that would be obscured by a conventional 1D reduction. All these affects are present in the calculated image, so the overlay tests the complete experimental model.
