\section{Refinement workflow}


With the diffraction model defined in Sec.~\ref{sec:diffraction_model}, we employ a staged refinement strategy to decouple the pixel-to-$\mathbf{Q}$ mapping from intensity-bearing parameters and to limit covariance (Table~\ref{tab:param_effects}). Instrument and beam parameters are established from calibration measurements, and the detector geometry is refined using a powder standard.

\begin{table}[!ht]
  \centering
  \caption{Parameters and their primary signatures in area detector images.}
  \label{tab:param_effects}
  \footnotesize
  \setlength{\tabcolsep}{5pt}
  \renewcommand{\arraystretch}{1.15}
  \begin{tabularx}{\linewidth}{@{}l L L@{}}
    \toprule
    Group & Parameters & What they control \\
    \midrule
    Instrument and beam &
    Detector geometry $D,\beta,\gamma,\chi,x_0,y_0$; beam widths $w_x,w_z$ and divergences $\Delta\theta_x,\Delta\theta_z$ &
    Fix the pixel-to-$\mathbf{Q}$ mapping and arc orientation, and set the instrument-limited radial width. \\
    Sample alignment &
    $\theta_i,\delta,z_B,z_S$; sample dimensions $W,H,t$; goniometer misalignment $\alpha,\psi$ &
    Set footprint and absorption weighting, shift features across the detector, and bias specular arc curvature. \\
    Mosaicity and stacking &
    Mosaic kernel $(\eta,\Gamma)$; stacking-disorder parameter $f$ &
    Set tangential arc and cap widths and the persistence of specular intensity off condition; $f$ broadens along $Q_z$ and shapes Bragg-rod diffuse scattering. \\
    Structure factors &
    $\mathbf{r}_a,o_a,\Delta\mathbf{r}_a$; anisotropic Debye--Waller tensor $\mathbf{U}$ &
    Set reflection-to-reflection intensity ratios, suppress high-$Q$ intensity, and modulate the $L$ dependence along $Q_z$. \\
    \bottomrule
  \end{tabularx}
\end{table}


With instrument parameters held fixed, we refine sample alignment from the positions of representative specular and off-specular features and determine mosaicity from the corresponding arc and cap line shapes. Finally, with geometry and mosaicity fixed, we refine intensity-bearing parameters against the full 2D image; for disordered films, stacking-disorder parameters are refined primarily against the specular Bragg rods and their $Q_z$ dependence.
