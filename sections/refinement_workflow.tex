\section{Refinement workflow}

Refinement is staged in five steps (Table~\ref{tab:param_effects}) to decouple detector/sample geometry from physical scattering parameters, reducing correlations and improving identifiability. Table~\ref{tab:param_effects} summarizes the dominant image signatures that constrain each parameter group, while the steps below describe the acquisition and fitting procedure. Additional workflow detail is provided in the supporting information (Sec.~S1).

\begin{table}[!ht]
  \centering
  \caption{Staged refinement workflow. For each step we list the primary parameter group and the dominant image signatures used to constrain it from area-detector images.}
  \label{tab:param_effects}
  \scriptsize
  \setlength{\tabcolsep}{4pt}
  \renewcommand{\arraystretch}{1.05}
  \begin{tabularx}{\linewidth}{@{}c l L L@{}}
    \toprule
    Step & Stage & Parameters & Dominant image signatures \\
    \midrule
    1 &
    Beam characterization (direct beam) &
    Beam widths $w_{x,z}$; divergences $\Delta\theta_{x,z}$ &
    Spot centroid/width versus detector distance (instrument broadening). \\
    2 &
    Detector geometry calibration (3D powder standard) &
    Detector distance $D$; tilts $(\beta,\gamma)$; in-plane rotation $\chi$; beam center $(x_0,y_0)$ &
    Ring center/ellipticity and orientation (pixel-to-$\mathbf{Q}$ mapping). \\
    3 &
    Sample alignment and goniometer misalignment (2D powder film) &
    Sample geometry $(\theta_i,\delta,z_B,z_S)$; goniometer misalignment $(\alpha,\psi)$; film $(W,H,t)$ &
    Specular/off-specular positions; footprint truncation; absorption weighting. \\
    4 &
    Mosaicity from Bragg-peak shapes (two $\theta_i$ images) &
    Mosaic kernel $(\eta,\Gamma,\sigma)$ &
    Arc/cap widths and specular off-condition tails. \\
    5 &
    Refinement of structure (Bragg + diffuse) &
    Scale; structure-factor parameters ($o_a$, $\mathbf{r}_a$, $\Delta\mathbf{r}_a$, $\mathbf{U}$); disorder (if present) &
    Relative Bragg intensities; diffuse along specular (Bragg rods) and between peaks along $Q_z$. \\
    \bottomrule
  \end{tabularx}
\end{table}

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Beam characterization (direct beam).}
  Acquire direct-beam images at several detector distances.
  Fit the spot centroid and width versus distance to determine the incident-beam phase-space
  parameters used by the diffraction model (Sec.~\ref{sec:diffraction_model}).

  \item \textbf{Detector geometry calibration (3D powder standard).}
  Measure a polycrystalline calibrant (e.g., hBN).
  Fit multiple Debye--Scherrer rings in detector coordinates and refine detector distance, tilts,
  in-plane rotation, and beam center so the rings forward-project to concentric circles.

  \item \textbf{Sample alignment and goniometer misalignment (2D powder film).}
  Use a 2D powder film to seed alignment.
  With detector parameters fixed, refine sample alignment and goniometer misalignment by minimizing positional residuals
  between measured and simulated features spanning specular and off-specular regions.
  Include finite-footprint and absorption weighting using the film dimensions and thickness
  (Sec.~\ref{sec:diffraction_model}).

  \item \textbf{Mosaicity from Bragg-peak shapes.}
  Acquire two images at different $\theta_i$ and fit them simultaneously using shared mosaic parameters in
  the misorientation model of Sec.~\ref{sec:mosaicity}.
  Report Lorentzian FWHM $=2\Gamma$, Gaussian FWHM $=2\sqrt{2\ln 2}\,\sigma$, and mixing factor $\eta$.

  \item \textbf{Refinement of structure (Bragg + diffuse).}
  With geometry and mosaicity fixed, refine an overall scale and structural parameters (occupancies,
  anisotropic Debye--Waller terms, and allowed static displacements) in the structure-factor intensity model
  (Eq.~\eqref{eq:structure_factor}) against the full 2D images (including specular).
  When disorder is present, include disorder parameters and constrain them primarily using the diffuse
  intensity along specular (Bragg rods) and between Bragg peaks along $Q_z$.
\end{enumerate}
