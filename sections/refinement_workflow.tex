\section{Refinement workflow}

Refinement is staged in five steps (Table~\ref{tab:param_effects}) to decouple detector/sample geometry from physical scattering parameters, reducing correlations and improving identifiability. Additional workflow detail is provided in the supporting information (Sec.~S1).

\begin{table}[!ht]
  \centering
  \caption{Staged refinement steps and the parameters they primarily constrain from area-detector images.}
  \label{tab:param_effects}
  \footnotesize
  \setlength{\tabcolsep}{5pt}
  \renewcommand{\arraystretch}{1.15}
  \begin{tabularx}{\linewidth}{@{}c l L L@{}}
    \toprule
    Step & Stage & Parameters & What they control \\
    \midrule
    1 &
    Beam characterization (direct beam) &
    Beam widths $w_x,w_z$; divergences $\Delta\theta_x,\Delta\theta_z$ &
    Set the incident-beam phase space and instrument-limited broadening used by the diffraction model. \\
    2 &
    Detector geometry calibration (3D powder standard) &
    Detector distance $D$; tilts $(\beta,\gamma)$; in-plane detector rotation $\phi$; beam center $(x_0,y_0)$ &
    Fix the detector-to-reciprocal-space geometry (distance, angles, rotation, beam center) and the orientation of features on the detector. \\
    3 &
    Sample alignment and goniometer misalignment (2D powder film) &
    $\theta_i,\delta,z_B,z_S,\chi$; film dimensions $W,H,t$; goniometer misalignment $(\alpha,\psi)$ &
    Place specular and off-specular features, and set footprint and absorption weighting. \\

    Set Bragg-peak arc and cap widths and the persistence of specular intensity off condition. \\
    5 &

    Overall scale; $\mathbf{r}_a,o_a,\Delta\mathbf{r}_a$; anisotropic Debye--Waller tensor $\mathbf{U}$; disorder parameters (if present) &

    \bottomrule
  \end{tabularx}

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Beam characterization (direct beam).}
  Acquire direct-beam images at several detector distances.
  Fit the spot centroid and width versus distance to determine the incident-beam phase-space
  parameters used by the diffraction model (Sec.~\ref{sec:diffraction_model}).

  \item \textbf{Detector geometry calibration (3D powder standard).}
  Measure a polycrystalline calibrant (e.g., hBN).
  Fit multiple Debye--Scherrer rings in detector coordinates and refine detector distance, tilts,
  in-plane rotation, and beam center so the rings forward-project to concentric circles.

  \item \textbf{Sample alignment and goniometer misalignment (2D powder film).}
  Use the 2D-powder signature (paired symmetric in-plane features and a specular feature) to seed alignment.
  With detector parameters fixed, refine sample alignment and goniometer misalignment by minimizing positional residuals
  between measured and simulated features spanning specular and off-specular regions.
  Include finite-footprint and absorption weighting using the film dimensions and thickness
  (Sec.~\ref{sec:diffraction_model}).

  \item \textbf{Mosaicity from Bragg-peak shapes.}
  Acquire two images at different $\theta_i$ and fit them simultaneously using shared mosaic parameters in
  the misorientation model of Sec.~\ref{sec:mosaicity}.
  Determine parameters from Bragg-peak arc/cap widths and specular off-condition tails after normalizing a
  single overall scale. Report Lorentzian FWHM $=2\Gamma$, Gaussian FWHM $=2\sqrt{2\ln 2}\,\sigma$, and
  mixing factor $\eta$.

  \item \textbf{Refinement of structure (Bragg + diffuse).}
  With geometry and mosaicity fixed, refine an overall scale and structural parameters (occupancies,
  anisotropic Debye--Waller terms, and allowed static displacements) in the structure-factor intensity model
  (Eq.~\eqref{eq:structure_factor}) against the full 2D images (including specular), matching
  reflection-to-reflection intensity ratios across both images.
  When disorder is present, include disorder parameters and constrain them primarily using the diffuse
  intensity along specular (Bragg rods) and between Bragg peaks along $Q_z$.
\end{enumerate}
