\section{Refinement workflow}

Refinement is staged to separately determine the detector and sample geometry and then refine physical scattering parameters. By breaking the procedure into five steps (Table~\ref{tab:param_effects}), we reduce parameter interdependence and improve identifiability.

  \centering
  \caption{Staged refinement steps and the parameters they primarily constrain from area-detector images.}
  \label{tab:param_effects}
  \footnotesize
  \setlength{\tabcolsep}{5pt}

  \begin{tabularx}{\linewidth}{@{}c l L L@{}}

    \midrule
    1 &

    2 &

    3 &
    Sample alignment and goniometer misset (2D powder film) &

  \end{tabularx}

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Beam characterization (direct beam).}
  Acquire direct-beam images at several detector distances.
  Fit the spot position and shape versus distance to determine the incident-beam widths
  $(w_x,w_z)$ and Gaussian divergences $(\Delta\theta_x,\Delta\theta_z)$ that set the
  instrument-limited broadening.

  \item \textbf{Detector geometry calibration (3D powder standard).}
  Measure a polycrystalline calibrant (e.g., hBN).
  Fit multiple Debye--Scherrer rings in detector coordinates and refine detector tilts
  $(\beta,\gamma)$, in-plane detector rotation $\phi$, and beam center $(x_0,y_0)$ so the rings
  forward-project to concentric circles.

  \item \textbf{Sample alignment and goniometer misset (2D powder film).}
  Use the 2D-powder signature (paired symmetric in-plane features and a near-specular feature) to seed alignment.
  With detector parameters fixed, refine sample alignment by minimizing positional residuals
  between measured and simulated features spanning specular and off-specular regions.
  Refine $(\theta_i,\delta,z_B,z_S,\chi)$ and the goniometer-axis misset rotations $(\alpha,\psi)$;
  include film dimensions $(W,H)$ and thickness $t$ to weight footprint and absorption.

  \item \textbf{Mosaicity from Bragg-peak shapes}
  Acquire two images at different $\theta_i$ and fit them simultaneously using shared mosaic parameters
  \[
    \omega(\Delta\theta)=
    \eta\,\frac{1}{\pi}\frac{\Gamma}{\Delta\theta^2+\Gamma^2}+
    (1-\eta)\,\frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}\sigma}\exp\!\left(-\frac{\Delta\theta^2}{2\sigma^2}\right),
    \qquad 0\le \eta \le 1,
  \]
  (use the $2\pi$-periodic wrapped form on $(-\pi,\pi]$ if implemented).
  Determine parameters from Bragg-peak arc and cap widths and specular off-condition tails after normalizing
  an overall scale. Report Lorentzian FWHM $=2\Gamma$, Gaussian FWHM $=2\sqrt{2\ln 2}\,\sigma$,
  and mixing factor $\eta$.

  \item \textbf{Refinement of structure (Bragg + diffuse).}
  With geometry and mosaicity fixed, refine the structural intensity model against the full 2D images
  (including near-specular).
  Evaluate all allowed reciprocal vectors $G$ explicitly and use $|F_G|^2$ to set Bragg reflection strengths.
  Refine an overall scale plus structural parameters (occupancies, anisotropic Debye--Waller terms, and allowed
  static displacements) to match reflection-to-reflection intensity ratios across both images.
  When disorder is present, include disorder parameters and refine them primarily against the diffuse intensity
  along $Q_z$ near specular and between Bragg peaks (which broadens and redistributes intensity along $Q_z$,
  orthogonal to mosaic broadening).
\end{enumerate}
